Training Philosophy

I am considered a balanced trainer and place exceptionally high emphasis on positive reinforcement and providing the dog with the best possible learning environment and opportunities for success.

I work hard to help you recognize, understand and subsequently reduce the pressures we place on our dogs while focusing on building a solid history of successes. It‚Äôs been my experience that this can be accomplished through helping you ‚Äúsee‚Äù the learning environment and associated challenges through the eyes of your dog.

I use a variety of tools in my own training, primarily martingale collars, marker cues and the more advanced multiple marker cues to take advantage of the dog‚Äôs own willingness to learn and please.

In some cases, I have used Herm Springer pinch collars or e-collars. It is important to note that those tools are reserved for specific issues and only used under intense supervision, thoughtful consideration of all other training avenues already explored, the dog himself, the client‚Äôs own goals and unique circumstances, and with the support of certified canine behaviorists.

I DO NOT require my students to use any method they do not embrace, and I welcome and encourage open and honest communication between student and instructor. There is no bad question in a learning environment. At the end of the day, this is your learning journey and both you and your dog should benefit from every mile traveled.

PLEASE NOTE: I am not a canine behaviorist, but I will do my best to research and help you with any issue we encounter. However, If I do not feel I can adequately address a behavior issue I will work with you to find professionals with more intensive training, certification, and experience. My goal is to provide you with the best opportunity for success possible.
